% Abstract - % directly after { is important for correct indentation
\AtlasAbstract{%
This analysis measures azimuthal correlations of muon pairs
  that arise from decays of heavy-flavor hadrons in Pb+Pb and in $pp$ 
  collisions at 5.02 TeV. 
The measurement uses the entrire Pb+Pb dataset recorded in Run\,2,
  corresponding to an integrated luminosity of 1.94 $\mathrm{nb}^{-1}$.
The 5.02~TeV $pp$ data were recored during a reference run in 2017 and
  correspond to an integrated luminosity of 260 $\mathrm{pb}^{-1}$.
The distributions of muon-pairs in azimuthal angle separation $\Delta\phi$, 
  are measured, and corrected to account for combinatorial 
  (independently produces)  muon-pairs. 
The widths of the away-side ($\Delta\phi=\pi$) correlation are measured as a function 
  of collision centrality and pair-charge.
The modifications in the widths in Pb+Pb collisions are compared to the 
  $pp$ reference and quantified as a function of the collision centrality. 
The widths of the angular correlation does not show a 
  significant modification with centrality.
These measurements can provide insight into the question whether collisional 
  or radiative processes dominate the energy loss of HF quarks as they 
  traverse the Quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.
}
